4th Putnam 1941


 
 
Problem B6

f is a real valued function on [0, 1], continuous on (0, 1). Prove that ∫x=0x=1 ∫y=xy=1 ∫z=xz=y f(x) f(y) f(z) dz dy dx = 1/6 ( ∫x=0x=1 f(x) dx )3.

 

Solution

Let Sxyz be the points in the cube for which x ≤ y ≤ z, let Syxz be the points for which y ≤ x ≤ z and so on. Then the union of the six sets is the cube and the intersection of any two has measure zero. Also by changing the variables of integration we see that the integral of f(x) f(y) f(z) over each set is the same. Hence the integral over Sxzy is 1/6 of the integral over the cube. But the integral over Sxzy is just ∫x=0x=1 ∫y=xy=1 ∫z=xz=y f(x) f(y) f(z) dz dy dx and the integral over the cube is ( ∫x=0x=1 f(x) dx )3.
